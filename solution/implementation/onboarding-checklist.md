# Client Onboarding Checklist

> Use this checklist for every new client to ensure a consistent, professional onboarding experience. First impressions after the close matter as much as the close itself.

---

## Overview

The onboarding phase (days 1–14) sets the tone for the entire relationship. The goal is to:
1. Deliver a "quick win" within the first 14 days
2. Build the client's confidence that they made the right decision
3. Establish the systems needed to deliver ongoing results

---

## Pre-Onboarding (Before Day 1)

- [ ] Agreement signed and on file
- [ ] Invoice sent / payment received
- [ ] Welcome email sent within 2 hours of signing (see template below)
- [ ] Client added to CRM with status "Active"
- [ ] Onboarding call scheduled within 5 business days
- [ ] Intake form sent (collect logins, brand assets, key contacts)

---

## Intake Form — What to Collect

| Item | Why You Need It |
|------|----------------|
| Google Business Profile login | Direct management access |
| Website admin login (or WP/Wix) | For website edits |
| Facebook Page admin access | For social posting and ads |
| Instagram login | For content posting |
| Google Analytics access | For reporting |
| Practice management software (PMS) name | For new patient tracking context |
| Brand colors + logo files | For content creation |
| List of services offered | For profile and content optimization |
| Key contact (owner + front desk) | For communication |
| Billing contact + payment method | For invoicing |

## Day 1–3: Orientation

- [ ] Onboarding call completed (see call guide below)
- [ ] Access to all platforms received and verified
- [ ] Baseline audit completed and documented (use [`../audit/audit-framework.md`](../audit/audit-framework.md))
- [ ] Baseline metrics recorded:
  - [ ] Google star rating: ___
  - [ ] Review count: ___
  - [ ] Website PageSpeed: ___
  - [ ] Social media followers (FB / IG): ___

---

## Day 4–7: Foundation Work

- [ ] Google Business Profile updated:
  - [ ] Photos added/updated
  - [ ] Services list completed
  - [ ] Business description optimized
  - [ ] Existing unanswered reviews responded to
- [ ] Review request system configured (text/email automation)
- [ ] Social media content calendar built (first 30 days)

---

## Day 8–14: First Quick Win Delivered

The first quick win is critical — it proves value and builds trust.

**Target quick wins (choose 1–2 based on audit):**
- [ ] First batch of new Google review requests sent → reviews coming in
- [ ] Website booking button added / improved
- [ ] Homepage CTA updated
- [ ] First week of social content posted
- [ ] All negative reviews responded to

- [ ] Quick win report sent to client (email summary of what was done and initial results)

---

## Day 14: First Check-In Call

Agenda:
1. Review what's been done
2. Show early results (even small wins)
3. Preview the next 30 days
4. Address any questions or concerns
5. Collect early feedback

---

## Onboarding Call Guide (Day 1–3)

**Duration:** 45–60 minutes

**Agenda:**
1. Welcome and relationship framing (5 min)
2. Practice overview — hear their priorities in their words (15 min)
3. Access + intake review (10 min)
4. Walkthrough of what you'll do in the first 30 days (10 min)
5. Communication norms — how often, what to expect, who to contact (5 min)
6. Q&A (10 min)

**Key thing to communicate:**
> "Our goal in the first 30 days is to earn your trust with real results. We'll move fast on the quick wins, and we'll keep you updated on everything we're doing."
