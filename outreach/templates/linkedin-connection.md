# LinkedIn Outreach Templates

> LinkedIn works best with dentists and practice owners who are professionally active on the platform. Always engage before connecting.

---

## Phase 1: Connection Request (Without Note)

For most LinkedIn connections, send without a note — it has a higher acceptance rate. Save your message for after they accept.

---

## Phase 2: Connection Request (With Note — 300 character limit)

Use when you have a very specific reason to connect:

```
Hi [First Name] — I came across [Practice Name] while researching 
dental practices in [City]. I have something that might be useful for you.
```

```
[First Name], I noticed [specific thing about their practice]. 
I specialize in working with practices like yours — thought it 
was worth connecting.
```

---

## Phase 3: First Message After Connecting

Send within 24–48 hours of acceptance. Keep it short and lead with value.

### Template A — Free Resource

```
Hi [First Name],

Thanks for connecting!

I've been doing some research on dental practices in [City/Area] 
and noticed [specific observation about their practice].

I put together a free [resource name] that addresses exactly this — 
thought it might be useful regardless of whether we ever work together.

Here it is: [Link]

Happy to answer any questions it sparks.

[Your Name]
```

### Template B — Free Audit Offer

```
Hi [First Name],

Thanks for connecting!

I work with dental practices on [one-line description of what you do]. 
I've been looking at [Practice Name] and have a few specific observations 
I'd love to share.

Would you be open to a no-pressure 15-minute conversation where 
I share what I found? Worst case, you get a fresh perspective. 
Best case, you walk away with a few actionable ideas.

No pitch — just value.

[Your Name]
```

---

## Phase 4: LinkedIn Follow-Up (If No Response After 5–7 Days)

```
Hi [First Name],

Just circling back on my last message — wanted to make sure 
[free resource] was useful.

One thing I should have mentioned: [new specific insight about their practice].

Worth a quick chat if you're curious. No obligation.

[Your Name]
```

## Engaging With Their Content Before Messaging

**Don't message cold.** Engage first:

| Engagement type | Example |
|----------------|---------|
| Like a post | Any recent post — shows interest |
| Comment thoughtfully | Add value to their post in 1–2 sentences |
| Share their post | With a comment about why it's useful |

Example comment on a post about patient experience:
> "This is such an underrated point — patients notice every detail. 
> We've seen practices double their referral rate just by auditing 
> the first 60 seconds of the patient visit. Great post."

---

## LinkedIn InMail (Premium Feature)

Use InMail only when the prospect is not in your network and you can't find another contact point.

```
Subject: Free audit for [Practice Name]

Hi [First Name],

[Practice Name] came up while I was researching dental practices in [City]. 
I did a quick review of your online presence and noticed [specific finding].

I specialize in helping practices like yours [one-line outcome]. 
I'd love to send you a free [resource] — no agenda.

Would that be useful?

[Your Name]
```

---

## Tips for LinkedIn Outreach

- ✅ Personalize every message with something specific to their practice
- ✅ Keep initial messages under 100 words
- ✅ Lead with what you're giving, not what you're selling
- ✅ Engage with their content before DMing
- ❌ Don't send connection + immediate sales pitch
- ❌ Don't use the word "synergy"
- ❌ Don't send the same message twice
