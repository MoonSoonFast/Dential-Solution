# Step 2 — Research & Personalize Before You Reach Out

> Generic outreach gets ignored. Specific, relevant outreach gets responses. Spend 10–15 minutes researching each prospect before sending a single message.

---

## 🔬 The 5-Minute Practice Audit (Pre-Outreach Research)

Run through this checklist for each prospect. Your findings become the fuel for personalized, value-first outreach.

### 1. Google Business Profile
- [ ] Claimed and fully completed?
- [ ] Average star rating and total review count
- [ ] Are they responding to reviews (especially negative ones)?
- [ ] Photos updated in the last 6 months?
- [ ] Business hours and contact info accurate?
- [ ] Posts in the last 30 days?

**Look for:** Low rating, unanswered negative reviews, missing photos, no posts, outdated info

---

### 2. Website Review
- [ ] Does it load fast? (Test at [PageSpeed Insights](https://pagespeed.web.dev))
- [ ] Mobile-friendly?
- [ ] Clear call-to-action on homepage (Book Now / Call Us)?
- [ ] Online booking capability?
- [ ] Patient testimonials or before/after gallery?
- [ ] HIPAA privacy notice and SSL certificate (https://)?
- [ ] Blog or content published recently?

**Look for:** Slow load, no CTA, no online booking, no social proof, outdated design

---

### 3. Social Media Presence
- [ ] Facebook page exists and is active?
- [ ] Instagram presence?
- [ ] Last post date on each platform
- [ ] Engagement rate (likes/comments relative to followers)
- [ ] Content quality: educational, promotional, personal?

**Look for:** Inactive accounts, low engagement, no educational content

---

### 4. Reviews Analysis
- [ ] Google review count and star average
- [ ] Most recent review (positive and negative)
- [ ] Response rate to negative reviews
- [ ] Common themes in positive reviews (what do patients love?)
- [ ] Common themes in complaints (what's the practice struggling with?)

**Look for:** Unanswered complaints, recurring issues, outdated positive reviews

---

### 5. Competitive Landscape
- [ ] Who are their top 3 local competitors?
- [ ] How does their rating compare?
- [ ] What are competitors doing better?

**Look for:** Opportunities to position your solution vs. gaps vs. competitors

---

## 📝 Research Note Template

For each prospect, document your findings using this template:

```
Practice: [Name]
Contact: [Dr. / Manager Name]
Date Researched: [Date]

GOOGLE BUSINESS:
- Rating: [X.X stars] / [X reviews]
- Last review response: [Date or "Never"]
- Profile issues: [List issues]

WEBSITE:
- URL: [website.com]
- Mobile: [Yes/No]
- CTA present: [Yes/No]
- Online booking: [Yes/No]
- PageSpeed score: [X/100]
- Key issues: [List issues]

SOCIAL MEDIA:
- Facebook: [Active/Inactive] — last post: [Date]
- Instagram: [Active/Inactive] — last post: [Date]
- Key issues: [List]

REVIEW THEMES:
- Positive: [e.g., "friendly staff", "gentle hygienist"]
- Negative: [e.g., "long wait times", "billing confusion"]

PERSONALIZATION HOOK:
[The ONE most compelling observation to lead with]

RECOMMENDED VALUE ASSET TO OFFER:
[Free audit / checklist / report / guide — see value-first/ folder]
```

---

## 🎣 Finding Your Personalization Hook

Your opening line should reference something specific you observed about their practice. Examples:

| Observation | Personalization Hook |
|-------------|---------------------|
| 4.1 stars with unanswered negative reviews | "I noticed a few recent reviews mentioning wait times that went unanswered — I put together a quick guide on how to turn those into your best marketing asset." |
| No online booking on website | "I was trying to find your online booking link and couldn't — you might be losing 30–40% of after-hours appointment requests without it." |
| Last Instagram post was 8 months ago | "Your Instagram account has great photos but hasn't posted since August — I have a 30-day content calendar built specifically for dental practices that I'd love to send over." |
| High reviews but no website testimonial section | "You have 89 glowing Google reviews but none are showing on your website — here's a simple way to turn that social proof into new patient bookings." |
