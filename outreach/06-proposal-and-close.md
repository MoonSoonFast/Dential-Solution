# Step 6 — Proposal & Close

> By the time you present the proposal, value has already been established. The close is a natural next step — not a pressure moment.

---

## 🧠 The Value-First Closing Mindset

If you've followed this playbook:
- They've already received free value from you (Step 3–4)
- They've experienced your expertise firsthand (discovery call)
- They've told you their problems (you listened, not pitched)
- The proposal mirrors their language and solves their specific pains

The close is simply confirming what both parties already know makes sense.

---

## 📄 Proposal Structure

### Section 1: What We Heard (Mirrors Their Language)
Start by reflecting their situation back to them. This shows you were listening.

```
"Based on our conversation on [Date], here's what we understand about 
[Practice Name]'s current situation:

• [Pain 1 in their words]
• [Pain 2 in their words]  
• Goal: [Their stated goal in their words]
```

### Section 2: Recommended Solution
Present only the solution relevant to their needs — not your full menu.

```
"Based on what you shared, we recommend starting with:

1. [Solution component 1] — addresses [their pain 1]
2. [Solution component 2] — addresses [their pain 2]
3. [Solution component 3] — enables [their goal]
```

### Section 3: What You Can Expect
Quantify the outcome where possible. Be honest and conservative.

```
"Within 90 days, practices like yours typically see:
• [Measurable outcome 1]
• [Measurable outcome 2]
• [Qualitative outcome]
```

### Section 4: Investment
Be direct about pricing. Never bury it.

```
"The investment for this program is [price] per month, 
with a [X-month] initial commitment.

What's included: [bullet list of deliverables]
```

### Section 5: Next Steps
Simple, clear, friction-free.

```
"To get started, we need:
1. [Signature / agreement]
2. [Onboarding call date]
3. [Access credentials / intake form]

We can have [first deliverable] live within [timeframe]."
```

---

## 🗣️ Presenting the Proposal (Live or Async)

### Live Presentation (Preferred)

Present via video call, not email. Walk through each section. Pause frequently:
- "Does this accurately reflect what you shared with us?"
- "Is this the priority, or is there something more urgent?"
- "What questions do you have on this section?"

### Async Proposal (If They Prefer Email)

If they want to review async, send:
1. The PDF/document proposal
2. A short Loom video walkthrough (2–3 minutes)
3. A clear "reply by" date for the offer

## 💬 Handling Common Objections

### "It's too expensive"
```
"I understand — budget is always a real consideration. Can I ask what 
an investment that makes sense would look like for you? I want to see 
if there's a version of this that fits your situation."
```

### "We need to think about it"
```
"Of course. What specifically would you need to think through? Sometimes 
I can answer it right now — or point you to something that makes the 
decision clearer."
```

### "We're working with someone else"
```
"Makes sense. Can I ask what's working well with your current provider, 
and what you wish was different? I want to make sure I'm not proposing 
something redundant."
```

### "Now's not a good time"
```
"Totally fair. When would be a better time — and should I put something 
in the calendar now so we don't lose track of it?"
```
