# Discovery Call Script

> This script is a **guide**, not a word-for-word recitation. Know it well enough to have a natural conversation, not read from a page.

---

## Before the Call

- [ ] Research notes reviewed
- [ ] Free audit or value asset ready to screen share
- [ ] CRM open to take notes in real time
- [ ] Video camera on (builds trust)
- [ ] Quiet, professional background

---

## The Script

### 📌 Opening (2 minutes)

> "Hi [Name], [Your Name] here — so great to meet you! Thanks for making time.
>
> I have us for about [30/45] minutes — does that still work?
>
> Here's what I had in mind: I'd love to spend most of the time hearing about [Practice Name] and what's on your plate right now. Then I have a couple of things I put together specifically for you that I'd love to share. Sound good?"

---

### 📌 Warm-Up (2–3 minutes)

> "Before we dive in — how long have you been at [Practice Name]? And is this your practice or are you part of a larger group?"

*(Listen. Let them share. Show genuine interest.)*

---

### 📌 Situational Discovery (10–15 minutes)

**On current state:**
> "What does new patient acquisition look like for you right now — what's working?"

**On challenges:**
> "And what's the biggest friction point when it comes to growth right now? Like, if you could wave a wand..."

**On past attempts:**
> "Have you tried anything to address that? What happened?"

**On goals:**
> "When you think about 12 months from now — what does a win look like for [Practice Name]?"

**On decision:**
> "If we found the right solution today, how do decisions like that typically get made at [Practice Name]?"

*(Take notes in real time. Mirror their language. Don't interrupt.)*

---

### 📌 Transition to Value Presentation (1 minute)

> "This is really helpful. Based on what you've shared — and honestly, based on some research I did before our call — I have something specific I'd like to show you. Mind if I share my screen for just a couple of minutes?"

---

### 📌 Value Delivery (5–8 minutes)

Walk through your pre-prepared audit or free asset:

1. **What I found:** "[Practice Name] has [specific observation]"
2. **What it means:** "This is likely costing you [quantified impact]"
3. **What a fix looks like:** "Here's what I'd recommend..."

Pause after each section:
> "Does this match what you're experiencing?"

---

### 📌 Gauging Interest (2 minutes)

> "How does this land for you? Is this the right area to be focused on?"

*(If yes → move to close. If mixed → explore more. If no → pivot or qualify out.)*

---

### 📌 Closing the Call (3–5 minutes)

> "Based on everything you've shared, I think there's a real path forward here. What I'd like to do is put together a specific proposal for [Practice Name] that tackles [top pain] and [second pain] — and walks through exactly how we'd get you to [their stated goal].
>
> Would it be useful to get on a call next week to walk through it? I could have it ready by [Day]."

*(Get a day/time committed before ending the call.)*

---

### 📌 Closing Line

> "Awesome. I'll send you a calendar invite and a quick summary of what we covered today. Really appreciated the conversation — talk soon."
