# Patient Acquisition Guide for Dental Practices

> A practical guide to growing your new patient count through digital channels. Use this as a value-first resource to share with prospects.

---

## Why Patient Acquisition Matters

The average dental practice needs **20–40 new patients per month** to maintain steady growth after natural attrition (patients who move, age out, or switch providers). Without a system, growth is inconsistent — dependent on word-of-mouth and luck.

This guide outlines the 6 most effective patient acquisition channels and how to activate each one.

---

## Channel 1: Google Local Search (Highest ROI)

**How it works:** When someone searches "dentist near me," Google shows 3 local results (the "map pack") before any organic results. Being in that top 3 is the single biggest driver of new patient calls.

**How to get there:**
1. Claim and fully optimize your Google Business Profile
2. Get to 50+ reviews with a 4.3+ star average
3. Ensure consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across all directories
4. Add photos, services, and posts regularly
5. Answer questions in the Q&A section

**Realistic outcome:** Practices in the Google map pack get 3–5x more calls than those outside it.

---

## Channel 2: Google Ads (Fastest Results)

**How it works:** Pay-per-click ads show at the top of Google when someone searches for a dentist. You only pay when someone clicks.

**What to target:** "Dentist [city]," "emergency dentist [city]," "teeth whitening [city]," etc.

**What to expect:**
- Average cost per click: $8–$25
- Average conversion rate (click → call): 5–15%
- Average cost per new patient lead: $30–$80

**When to use it:** When you want new patients fast and have a budget. Works best alongside strong organic presence.

---

## Channel 3: Your Website (Your 24/7 Salesperson)

**The biggest leaks:**
- No online booking → patients go elsewhere after hours
- No CTA above the fold → visitors leave without taking action
- Slow mobile load → 50%+ of visitors bounce
- No social proof → visitors don't trust the practice yet

**The quick fixes:**
1. Add an online booking widget (Zocdoc, NexHealth, or built-in PMS)
2. Put "Book Online" and phone number in the header on every page
3. Add at least 5 patient testimonials to the homepage
4. Run a PageSpeed test and fix the top 3 issues

**Realistic outcome:** Optimized website conversions can double or triple new patient form submissions.

---

## Channel 4: Online Reviews (Trust Engine)

**The numbers:**
- 84% of patients trust online reviews as much as a personal recommendation
- Practices with 50+ reviews and 4.5+ stars get 2–3x more clicks than those with fewer
- A single unanswered negative review can cost a practice 15–30 prospective patients per month

**How to build a review system:**
1. Ask every satisfied patient at checkout: "Would you be willing to leave us a Google review?"
2. Send a text follow-up with a direct Google review link (2 hours after appointment)
3. Respond to EVERY review within 7 days

---

## Channel 5: Social Media (Long-Term Trust)

**The goal:** Stay top-of-mind with existing patients and attract new ones through organic content.

**What works for dental practices:**
- Educational content ("How to floss correctly," "Signs you need a night guard")
- Before/after photos (with patient consent)
- Team spotlights and behind-the-scenes
- Patient testimonials (video or written, with consent)
- Seasonal content (back-to-school, holiday specials)

**Minimum viable social strategy:**
- 3 posts/week on Instagram and/or Facebook
- 1 educational, 1 social/team, 1 promotional
- Respond to all comments and DMs within 24 hours

---

## Channel 6: Patient Referrals (Highest Quality Patients)

**The math:** Referred patients have a 37% higher retention rate and a 25% higher lifetime value than non-referred patients.

**How to activate referrals:**
1. Ask directly: "If you know anyone looking for a dentist, we'd be grateful for the recommendation."
2. Create a formal referral program (e.g., a gift card for every referred patient who books)
3. Make it easy: give patients a referral card at checkout
4. Follow up with a thank-you note when a referral comes in
